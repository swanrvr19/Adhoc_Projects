## Episode Summary

The All-In Podcast hosted a "Best Ideas Pitch Competition" inspired by the historic Ira Sohn Investment Conference, where four professional investors presented their top investment ideas to a live audience. The event was introduced by Chamath Palihapitiya, who recalled his own experiences at the original Sohn Conference, including his successful Amazon trillion-dollar prediction in 2015 and his Tesla convertibles pick in 2016, as well as his less successful Box pick in 2017 when he meant to bet on AI via Nvidia.

The first pitcher was Aaron Cowan of Seretta Capital, a $4 billion generalist hedge fund, who pitched MGM Resorts as a potential triple. His thesis centered on Barry Diller's aggressive 26% stake accumulation and recent $48/share bid, combined with two "hidden assets": a licensed casino in Osaka, Japan (opening ~2030) positioned to attract Chinese gamblers, and a potential casino opportunity in Dubai where MGM has strategically left 300,000 square feet of empty space in a new development. Cowan argued the Vegas assets plus China were worth ~$60, Japan added ~$50, and Dubai could add another $40-50 per share.

Daniel Drefus pitched Talon Energy, a power producer with 2 gigawatts of nuclear and 6 gigawatts of natural gas capacity, arguing it could be purchased at a $25 billion enterprise value versus $45 billion replacement cost. He laid out the structural case for a massive power demand cycle driven by AI data centers and broader electrification, noting that even without AI, power markets would remain tight for 20 years. He presented three scenarios ranging from $50/share to $100+ in free cash flow, against a stock price in the high $300s.

Oleg Nodelman of Eco R1 Capital, a value-oriented biotech fund that has 10x'd investor capital since 2013, pitched Actis Oncology (AKTS), a $1 billion market cap radiopharmaceutical company. He used military warfare analogies to explain the evolution of cancer treatment, positioning Actis's mini-protein platform as "micro drones" that deliver radioactive payloads with extreme precision. He highlighted the company's $500 million enterprise value, 3+ years of cash runway, Eli Lilly's $100 million backstop on their oversubscribed IPO, and upcoming clinical data readouts in 2027.

Kyle Samani, founder of Multicoin Capital, pitched Geonet (GEOD token), a decentralized real-time kinematics (RTK) network that provides ~2 centimeter location precision versus GPS's ~2 meter accuracy. He explained how the network grew to 22,000 base stations across 150 countries through a crypto-incentive model, surpassing legacy competitors Trimble, Hexagon, and Topcon combined. The token trades at ~$150 million fully diluted, with $11 million in annualized revenue growing 3x year-over-year, and 80% of revenue used to buy tokens on the open market.

In the final voting, the audience chose Daniel Drefus (Talon Energy) as the winner with 50% of the vote, while the besties ranked Aaron Cowan (MGM) first, creating a split decision. The hosts debated the merits of each pitch, with general consensus that MGM and Talon offered the best risk-adjusted returns with downside protection, while Actis and Geonet represented higher-risk lottery tickets with potential for outsized returns.

## Key Takeaways

- **MGM Resorts has significant hidden value** beyond its Vegas properties: a licensed Osaka casino (opening 2030) that could tap into the massive Asian gambling market, and a potential Dubai casino option, with Barry Diller's $48/share bid providing a floor.
- **Power demand is entering a structural supercycle** that doesn't even require AI to play out, but AI turbocharges the shortage; existing power assets trading below replacement cost represent compelling investments.
- **Talon Energy trades at roughly half its replacement cost** ($25B EV vs $45B replacement), with multiple scenarios for free cash flow growth from $50/share to $100+/share against a stock price in the high $300s.
- **Radiopharmaceuticals represent a new frontier in cancer treatment** with a natural moat — radioactive isotopes are difficult to replicate generically and are off-limits to China due to different nuclear program histories.
- **Geonet has built the world's largest RTK network** in just 4 years using a decentralized crypto-incentive model, achieving 2x the base stations of the three largest legacy competitors combined.
- **The Sam Zell investment framework** of buying hard assets below replacement cost that will be needed in the future remains a powerful approach, particularly for power infrastructure.
- **Regulatory risk is the biggest concern for power/AI investments**, as governments may intervene on pricing or allocation of electricity between data centers and consumers.
- **Biotech investing requires focusing on risk-reward rather than falling in love with science**, and platform companies that can demonstrate broad applicability command the highest valuations.
- **GLP-1/obesity drugs may be among the most important longevity drugs ever developed**, as caloric restriction is one of the few proven life-extension interventions.
- **Liquidity and position sizing matter** as much as the quality of the idea — great investments in small markets (like crypto tokens) may be impractical for large allocators.
