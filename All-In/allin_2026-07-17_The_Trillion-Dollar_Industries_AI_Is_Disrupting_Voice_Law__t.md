## Episode Summary

This episode of the All-In Podcast features two in-depth interviews focused on how AI is disrupting trillion-dollar industries. The first interview is with Mati, CEO and co-founder of 11 Labs, the AI voice technology company that has achieved extraordinary revenue growth — from zero to $600 million in ARR in roughly 40-50 months. Mati discusses the company's culture of small teams with embedded engineers, its decision to never hire product managers, and how the company maintains quality while scaling rapidly. The conversation covers the evolution of voice AI from painful "voice jail" experiences to natural, human-like interactions that consumers now prefer over speaking with human agents.

The 11 Labs discussion delves into critical issues around voice identity and IP protection, including the company's three-pronged safety approach: tracing all generated content, moderating at both voice and text levels, and building AI detection systems. Notable partnerships include work with Matthew McConaughey, the estate of James Earl Jones (powering Darth Vader's voice in Fortnite with Epic Games and Disney), Headspace for meditation content, and MasterClass for interactive celebrity-taught courses. Mati also addresses heartwarming use cases like restoring voices for ALS patients and Congresswoman Jennifer Wexton. On competition with frontier model companies like OpenAI and Anthropic, Mati emphasizes 11 Labs' model-agnostic platform approach while noting they are exploring building their own interaction-focused models.

The second interview features Max, CEO of Legora (also referred to as Lora), a legal AI company that has achieved 50% quarter-over-quarter growth for seven consecutive quarters, becoming one of the fastest enterprise companies with a direct sales motion to reach $100-150 million. Max discusses how AI is fundamentally reshaping the $1 trillion legal services industry, where only 4% of spend goes to software versus 96% to manual services. He describes Legora's approach of forward-deploying "legal engineers" (similar to Palantir's forward-deployed engineers) to help law firms transition from pre-AI to post-AI operations, and how the company is building comprehensive legal data across all jurisdictions worldwide.

The legal AI discussion addresses the disruption of legacy players like LexisNexis and Westlaw, the transformation of the billable hour model, and how junior lawyer roles will evolve. Max explains that Legora has acquired four businesses so far this year, conducting diligence in-house with their own tools — completing one transaction in just 12 days from LOI to closing. He argues against building general legal intelligence models, instead favoring narrow, fine-tuned models for specific use cases, and emphasizes that compliance is Legora's currency, hosting national secrets and weapons manufacturers' contracts on their platform.

## Key Takeaways

- **11 Labs has reached $600M in ARR** with only 600 employees, achieving remarkable capital efficiency and zero attrition among the original 10 team members.
- **11 Labs has never hired product managers**, instead optimizing for profiles that are expert in at least one field (coding, customer understanding, design) and competent in others, with AI bridging skill gaps.
- **Voice AI has passed a turning point** where consumers now prefer interacting with AI agents over humans, feeling less embarrassed and able to communicate more efficiently without social friction.
- **11 Labs has paid over $22 million** back to voice creators through its marketplace, creating a new revenue model for voiceover talent.
- **The legal services industry is a $1 trillion market** with only 4% software penetration, representing an enormous opportunity for AI disruption.
- **Legora achieved 50% quarter-over-quarter growth** for seven consecutive quarters, becoming one of the fastest enterprise companies to reach $100-150M with a direct sales motion.
- **The billable hour model is being fundamentally challenged** — AI can compress 90% of legal work, shifting value from associate hours to partner expertise and judgment.
- **Legacy legal data providers** (LexisNexis, Westlaw) are struggling to pivot to AI-native businesses due to talent gaps, organizational politics, and slower execution tempo.
- **AI agents are moving from augmentation to autonomous work** in legal, with models like Opus 4.5/4.6 capable of end-to-end case strategy combining witness statements and case law.
- **Startups are already operating without corporate lawyers**, using ChatGPT for contracts, cap tables, HR, and even IP assignments — a trend that will reshape legal services demand.
