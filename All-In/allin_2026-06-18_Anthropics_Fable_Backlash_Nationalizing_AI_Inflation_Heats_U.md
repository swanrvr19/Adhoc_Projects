## Episode Summary

The episode opens with an extensive discussion of Anthropic's release of Fable 5, a new mythos-level AI model that tops nearly every benchmark but has sparked major developer backlash. The hosts detail how Anthropic stores all prompt data for at least 30 days, secretly downgrades users it suspects of conducting frontier AI research, and buries these policies deep in documentation. David Friedberg shares firsthand experience of genomics research being blocked by the model's overly broad safety restrictions, while David Sacks argues Anthropic is engaged in a sophisticated regulatory capture campaign designed to ultimately restrict open-source competitors. The hosts express concern that these restrictions are pushing companies toward Chinese open-source models, creating a national security and economic disadvantage for the United States. Jason Calacanis demonstrates live on air how the model downgraded him for asking simple questions about fertilizer regulations and nuclear components.

The conversation transitions to Bernie Sanders' proposed American AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act, which would impose a one-time 50% tax on stock of the largest AI companies. While all hosts oppose outright confiscation, Sacks expresses surprising sympathy for the proposal, arguing that AI companies have invited this reaction by repeatedly telling the public their technology will cause massive job loss while training on humanity's collective knowledge for free. Friedberg passionately argues there is no AI job loss, citing his own company's expansion and the strong May jobs report, and proposes instead reforming Social Security into a sovereign wealth fund that could invest in equities including AI companies. Chamath notes the enormous capital requirements for AI infrastructure, mentioning his own 2-3 gigawatt data center projects.

The hosts discuss concerning inflation data, with CPI at 4.2% year-over-year and PPI at 6.5% year-over-year, largely attributed to energy impacts from the Iran conflict. They note that despite the hot print, markets were up, suggesting the numbers were in line with expectations. The episode concludes with a heated discussion about the Los Angeles mayoral primary, where the hosts analyze statistical discrepancies between in-person voting (where Spencer Pratt led), pre-election mail-in ballots (where Karen Bass led), and post-election mail-in ballots (where Nithya Raman surged). The hosts argue California's ballot harvesting laws, lack of voter ID requirements, and dirty voter rolls create systemic vulnerabilities, with Friedberg framing it as a "system of appointment, not election" and calling for federal congressional action to mandate voter ID and proper registration.

## Key Takeaways

- Anthropic's Fable 5 model conducts mandatory 30-day surveillance of all prompts, secretly downgrades users researching AI/ML topics, and has sparked massive developer backlash for anti-competitive and deceptive practices.
- Anthropic's safety restrictions are pushing companies, including Friedberg's agricultural company, toward Chinese open-source models, creating potential national security risks for the US.
- Dario Amodei's simultaneous calls for AI regulation and hiring of researchers for recursive self-improvement are seen as deeply hypocritical and part of a regulatory capture strategy against open-source competitors.
- Bernie Sanders' proposal to seize 50% of AI company equity is viewed as confiscatory but somewhat justified given that AI companies have told the public their technology will destroy jobs while training on humanity's collective knowledge.
- Friedberg argues AI is not destroying jobs but rather expanding revenue capacity, with his own company hiring more engineers because AI enables them to build more products.
- Friedberg proposes reforming Social Security into a sovereign wealth fund that can invest in equities, including AI companies, rather than holding only Treasury certificates.
- CPI hit 4.2% and PPI hit 6.5% year-over-year, the highest levels since 2023 and 2022 respectively, driven partly by the Iran conflict's impact on energy prices.
- The LA mayoral primary showed statistically improbable swings between in-person and late mail-in ballots, with California's legalized ballot harvesting, lack of voter ID, and automatic ballot mailing creating systemic vulnerabilities.
- Thomas Leffont's data from Liquidity Summit showed the probability of reaching trillion-dollar market cap increases at each stage: 8% unicorn to decacorn, 13% decacorn to centicorn, 31% centicorn to trillion.
- The capital cost for AI infrastructure has exploded to approximately $100 billion per gigawatt of data center capacity, making open-source alternatives extremely capital-intensive.
